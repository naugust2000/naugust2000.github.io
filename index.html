Mental Health Awareness: Internet Addiction

The modern world is advancing rapidly. This is especially true when considering recent technological advancements. Society's reliance on personal devices and the internet is growing as a result. The internet now dominates our society. There are both positive and negative outlooks observable from this situation. Thus, we should use the internet with caution. The demand for mental health awareness in reference to internet addiction remains high.

Blog Post #1: Read this if You’ve Ever Felt Unhappy While Using Social Media

This article informs the public that the negative feelings associated with social media usage are not unusual. This issue, however, is rarely spoken of publicly. The article proceeds to give possible explanations for these negative emotions. By being aware of one's triggers, negative emotions are less likely to arise. Social media has the potential to increase interaction and provide positive emotions. This is only applicable, however, once people stop using these platforms to compare themselves to one another.

Blog Post #2: Curing Your Addiction to “Digital Cocaine”

This article aims to provide potential treatments for mild cases of internet addiction. Such remedies can be attempted at home without the use of therapy and are designed to limit, not restrict, internet usage.

The JVN Podcast: Podcast #1

The JVN podcast speaks of real world issues and stories associated with internet and video game addictions. The target audience are current parents who may have children at risk of an addiction. The podcast’s main goal is to raise awareness of these risks in order to see different warning signs in advance. The podcast provides additional parenting techniques that may alter the future outcome of a child. Such methods may inspire children find motivation within themselves.

Video: Cruel Game

"Cruel Game" is a video that uses emotional appeal to emphasize the detrimental effects of video game addictions. The addict harms himself mentally while he struggles to grasp onto reality. That is, he struggles to maintain social relationships, cleanliness, and consistent sleep patterns. Many of these examples are comparable with real world cases.

Seeking Out Help: The National Helpline

The national helpline is free to use and aims to help those in need. The service is available 24/7, 365 days a year. It aims to treat different addictions and mental disorders. Among the most significant resources is the suicide prevention hotline. Those struggling from an addiction are at a higher risk of self-harm than those who do not have one. For these individuals, it's important to know these resources are available at any given moment.

Seeking Out Help: Caron Treatment Programs

Caron, a free to use mental health service, aims to provide individuals with local treatment programs tailored to their needs. Among the disorders of treatment is internet addiction.
